How to Make Fried Rice

Description

Fried rice is the ultimate culinary chameleon, transforming humble leftover rice into a deeply comforting, high-heat masterpiece. Born out of a practical need to avoid food waste, the dish relies on cold, day-old grains, which lose just enough moisture to dry out and fry cleanly without turning into mush. When tossed in a ripping-hot wok with a quick splash of soy sauce, aromatic garlic, and whatever bits of vegetables, eggs, or proteins you have on hand, the rice undergoes the Maillard reaction—unlocking a savory, slightly smoky depth of flavor often referred to as wok hei (the breath of the wok). It is a dish that requires no rigid recipe, operating instead as a delicious canvas for culinary improvisation.

  • Refrigerated jasmine rice
  • Wok or frying pan
  • Oil of choice
  • Minced garlic
  • 1-2 eggs
  • Vegetables of choice. I.E, carrots, peas, baby corn
  • Light soy sauce
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Shaoxing cooking wine
